About

Art is often treated as an escape hatch. I don’t make work for that. I make work that brings people closer to what is here. Two ideas guide me: do all in the name of art, and let art be done by all.

There are too many untold stories. Too many bright minds without a canvas. Too many canvases waiting for a mind to meet them. I’m drawn to the moment where a vision becomes material—when an idea stops being private and starts taking up space in the world.

I grew up in small schools where creativity was treated as a discipline. I carried that forward. A straight path gets you there; a curved path shows you what else is possible. Over time, I kept noticing the same problem in talented people: they could see the thing clearly, but didn’t have a way to build it. My work is often about building that way.

In New Orleans, I expected to find completely new artistic thinking. Instead I found familiar doubts: it hasn’t been done before, people won’t like it, I don’t know how. I don’t accept those as final answers. The point of humanity is to create; the responsibility is to carry visions through. That is the center of my practice.

Education: B.A. in Theatre Arts & Psychology — Loyola University New Orleans. Pursuing an M.F.A. in Producing — CalArts.
